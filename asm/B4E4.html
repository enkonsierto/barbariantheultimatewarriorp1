B4E4: Hit type parameter table.
Indexed by hit type index from hit_detection_table. Each entry is 3 bytes: range byte, reaction movement ID, and event/knockback byte.
Byte 0: four 2-bit distance thresholds packed into one byte (bits 1-0 = field 0, bits 3-2 = field 1, bits 5-4 = field 2, bits 7-6 = field 3). collision_solver extracts each field and compares it against the fighter tile distance in fighter_distance. Any match validates the hit; all four misses discard it silently.
Byte 1: movement ID applied to the defender via force_apply_moves on a valid hit. 255 = no reaction.
Byte 2 high nibble: combat event type written to pending_sound_index (0 = no event). Low nibble: knockback type passed to energy_dec (0 = no energy decrement).
Types 0-3: stagger/fall, kick/slash, with energy damage.
Types 4-5: mv8 decapitation, headcut event, no energy damage.
Type 6: no reaction, guard2 event, no energy damage (full block).
Types 7-12: fall, kick/tackle, with or without energy damage.
Types 13-16: no reaction or stagger, guard1/guard2/slash, with or without damage.
Types 17-18: fall, tackle event, NO energy damage (roll forward hits).
Types 19-24: fall, slash event, with energy damage.
Types 25-31: stagger, slash event, with energy damage (range variants).
Types 32-35: fall, slash event, with energy damage (overhead slash exclusive).
Types 36-38: stagger, slash event, with energy damage (range variants, top/headcut area).
Types 32-35 are used exclusively by the overhead slash attacker frame (R45740): the only move that produces mv25 fall with slash event instead of kick event.
